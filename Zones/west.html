Statue of Liberty

USA

Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty, located on Liberty Island in New York Harbor, is a symbol of freedom and democracy. A gift from France to the United States, it was designed by sculptor Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi and is made of copper. Standing at 46 meters tall, the statue holds a torch and a tablet inscribed with the date of the American Declaration of Independence.

Date of construction: Dedicated on October 28, 1886.

Colossus of Rhodes

Greece

Colossus of Rhodes

The Colossus of Rhodes was a giant statue of the sun god Helios, erected in Rhodes, Greece, to celebrate the island’s victory over Cyprus in 305 BCE. Standing approximately 33 meters tall, it was one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. The statue was destroyed by an earthquake in 226 BCE, and its remains were eventually sold off as scrap metal...

Date of construction: Completed in 292 BCE and destroyed in 226 BCE.

Mount Rushmore

USA

Mount Rushmore

Mount Rushmore, located in the Black Hills of South Dakota, USA, features the colossal carvings of four U.S. presidents: George Washington, Thomas Jefferson, Theodore Roosevelt, and Abraham Lincoln. Designed by sculptor Gutzon Borglum, the monument symbolizes the birth, growth, development, and preservation of the United States. It attracts millions of visitors annually and is an iconic symbol of American heritage...

Date of construction: Began in 1927 and completed in 1941.

Grand Canyon

USA

Grand Canyon

The Grand Canyon, located in Arizona, USA, is a massive geological formation carved by the Colorado River over millions of years. Stretching 277 miles in length and reaching depths of over 6,000 feet, it is known for its breathtaking landscapes and rich history. The Grand Canyon is home to various ecosystems and significant archaeological sites, making it a natural wonder of the world...

Date of construction: The canyon's formation began around 5 to 6 million years ago, though its geological layers date back over 2 billion years.

Yellowstone National Park

USA

Yellowstone National Park

Yellowstone National Park, located primarily in Wyoming, USA, is the first national park in the world, known for its geothermal features, diverse wildlife, and stunning landscapes. The park sits on top of a supervolcano, featuring famous attractions like Old Faithful, Grand Prismatic Spring, and Yellowstone Lake. It is home to a variety of species, including grizzly bears, wolves, and bison..

Date of construction: Established on March 1, 1872.

Niagara Falls

Canada/USA

Niagara Falls

Niagara Falls, located on the border between Canada and the United States, is a world-famous waterfall known for its stunning beauty and massive flow of water. It consists of three waterfalls: Horseshoe Falls, American Falls, and Bridal Veil Falls. The falls attract millions of tourists annually and are also a significant source of hydroelectric power..

Date of construction: The falls formed around 12,000 years ago at the end of the last ice age.

Golden Gate Bridge

USA

Golden Gate Bridge

The Golden Gate Bridge is an iconic suspension bridge connecting San Francisco to Marin County, California. Known for its distinctive International Orange color and art deco design, the bridge spans the Golden Gate Strait, the entrance to the San Francisco Bay. It is considered one of the most photographed structures in the world and an engineering marvel..

Date of construction: Completed on April 19, 1937.

Hollywood Sign

USA

Hollywood Sign

The Hollywood Sign is an iconic symbol of the entertainment industry, located in the Hollywood Hills of Los Angeles, California. Originally created as an advertisement for a real estate development, it has become synonymous with the film industry and Los Angeles. The sign stands 45 feet tall and spans 352 feet in length, attracting millions of visitors each year..

Date of construction: Originally installed in 1923, and restored in 1978.
