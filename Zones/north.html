Stonehenge

United Kingdom

Taj Mahal

Stonehenge is a prehistoric monument located in Wiltshire, England, known for its massive stone circle. It is believed to have been constructed in several stages, with the earliest phase dating back to around 3000 BC, and the main stone circle being erected around 2500 BC. The purpose of Stonehenge remains a subject of mystery, but it is thought to have had religious, astronomical, or ceremonial significance..

Date of construction : 3000 BC - 2500 BC

Eiffel Tower

Paris

Taj Mahal

The Eiffel Tower is a wrought-iron lattice tower located on the Champ de Mars in Paris, France. Designed by engineer Gustave Eiffel, its construction began in 1887 and was completed in 1889 for the 1889 Exposition Universelle (World's Fair) held to celebrate the centenary of the French Revolution. Standing at 330 meters (1,083 feet), it was the tallest man-made structure in the world until the completion of the Chrysler Building in New York in 1930..

Date of construction : 1887-1889

Colosseum

Italy

Colosseum

The Colosseum is an ancient amphitheater located in the center of Rome, Italy. It was commissioned by Emperor Vespasian of the Flavian dynasty and was completed by his son Titus in 80 AD, with further modifications made during the reign of Domitian. The Colosseum was primarily used for gladiatorial contests and public spectacles, such as animal hunts, mock sea battles, and executions. It remains one of the most iconic landmarks of ancient Rome..

Acropolis

Greece

Acropolis

The Acropolis of Athens is an ancient citadel located on a rocky outcrop above the city of Athens, Greece. It contains several significant buildings, the most famous of which is the Parthenon, a temple dedicated to the goddess Athena. The construction of the Parthenon began in 447 BC and was completed in 438 BC. The Acropolis was a symbol of ancient Greek civilization and democracy and remains a major archaeological and tourist site..

Date of construction : 447-438 BC (for the Parthenon)

Notre-Dame Cathedral

France

Petra

The Notre-Dame Cathedral is a famous Gothic cathedral located in Paris, France, on the Île de la Cité. Construction began in 1163 AD under the reign of King Louis VII and was completed in 1345 AD. The cathedral is renowned for its stunning architecture, including its flying buttresses, rose windows, and intricate sculptures. It has served as an important religious, cultural, and historical symbol for France..

Date of construction : 1163-1345 AD

The Kremlin

Russia

The Kremlin

The Kremlin is a historic fortified complex located in the center of Moscow, Russia. It serves as the official residence of the President of Russia and has been a symbol of Russian power and governance for centuries. The construction of the Kremlin began in the 2nd half of the 15th century under the orders of Ivan III, though it has been expanded and modified over time. The Kremlin contains several significant buildings, including cathedrals, the Grand Kremlin Palace, and the famous Spasskaya Tower..

Big Ben

united Kingdom

Big Ben

Big Ben is the nickname for the Great Bell of the Elizabeth Tower at the Palace of Westminster in London, England. The construction of the tower and the clock began in 1843 and was completed in 1859. The bell, which weighs over 13 tons, was first struck in 1859, and the tower itself has since become one of the most recognizable landmarks in the world..

Date of construction : 1843-1859
