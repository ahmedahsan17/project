Taj Mahal

India

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is a mausoleum built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. Completed in 1653, it is an exquisite example of Mughal architecture, combining Islamic, Persian, Ottoman, and Indian styles. Made of white marble, it symbolizes eternal love and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Date of construction: Began in 1632 and completed in 1653.

Great Wall Of China

China

Great Wall Of China

The Great Wall of China is a historic fortification that stretches over 13,000 miles across northern China. Initially built to protect against invasions, it was constructed by several dynasties, primarily the Qin, Han, and Ming Dynasties. The wall is an incredible feat of engineering, featuring watchtowers, barriers, and strategic routes. It is a symbol of China's strength and endurance.

Date of construction: Began in 7th century BC and was extended until the 17th century.

Angkor Wat

Cambodia

Angkor Wat

Angkor Wat, located in Cambodia, is the largest religious monument in the world. Originally constructed as a Hindu temple dedicated to the god Vishnu, it was later transformed into a Buddhist temple. Known for its stunning architecture and intricate carvings, it represents the classical style of Cambodian architecture and is a symbol of Cambodia's cultural heritage.

Date of construction: Completed in the early 12th century during the reign of King Suryavarman II.

Petra

Jordan

Petra

Petra, an ancient city in Jordan, is renowned for its rock-cut architecture and water management system. Founded by the Nabataeans around the 6th century BCE, Petra became a major trading hub. Its most famous feature is the Al-Khazneh (Treasury), carved directly into the rose-red cliffs. Today, Petra is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.

Date of construction: Founded around the 6th century BCE.

Machu Picchu

Peru

Machu Picchu

Machu Picchu, an ancient Incan city located in the Andes Mountains of Peru, is known for its stunning terraces, temples, and palaces. Believed to have been built in the 15th century during the reign of Emperor Pachacuti, it remained hidden from the outside world until its rediscovery in 1911 by explorer Hiram Bingham. Today, it is one of the most iconic archaeological sites in the world.

Date of construction: Built in the 15th century.

Mount Fuji

Japan

Mount Fuji

Mount Fuji, located in Japan, is an active stratovolcano and the country's highest peak, standing at 3,776 meters. It is an iconic symbol of Japan, revered for its beauty and spiritual significance. Mount Fuji is a popular site for hiking and is depicted in numerous artworks, particularly in Japanese ukiyo-e woodblock prints. It is also considered a sacred site in Shinto and Buddhist traditions.

Date of construction: 1707, during the Edo period.

Temple of Heaven

China

Temple of Heaven

The Temple of Heaven in Beijing, China, is an ancient religious complex used by emperors of the Ming and Qing Dynasties to perform annual ceremonies for a good harvest. The temple is renowned for its architectural design and symbolism, representing the relationship between heaven and earth. Its most famous structure is the Hall of Prayer for Good Harvests, an elegant circular building with a distinctive blue-tiled roof.

Date of construction: Completed in 1420 during the Ming Dynasty.

Shwedagon Pagoda

Myanmar

Shwedagon Pagoda

The Shwedagon Pagoda, located in Yangon, Myanmar, is one of the most famous and revered Buddhist stupas in the world. It is said to house relics of the Buddha and has a golden stupa that towers at 99 meters. The pagoda is surrounded by numerous smaller stupas and shrines, making it a center of spiritual and cultural significance for Buddhists. Its golden exterior is a symbol of purity and devotion...

Date of construction: Believed to have been built around the 6th century BCE, with renovations over the centuries.
